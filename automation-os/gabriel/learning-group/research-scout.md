# Research Scout Workflow

## Mission

Find one useful thing. Not ten interesting things. One useful, specific, evidence-backed thing that Gabriel can act on.

## Before Starting

1. Confirm the topic from `RESEARCH_TOPICS.md`
2. Confirm the business lane it applies to
3. Set a 20-minute time limit on research. Stop at 20 minutes regardless.
4. Load the relevant framework (TOP_COMPANY_FRAMEWORK, TOP_EARNER_FRAMEWORK, or SPORTS_BRANDING_FRAMEWORK) if needed

## Research Protocol

### For Marketing/Conversion/Lead-Gen Topics
1. Search top-tier sources first (`RESEARCH_SOURCES.md` — High tier only)
2. Find one specific, named example — not general advice
3. Extract the data or process behind the example
4. Stop when you have one strong source

### For Elite Company Topics
1. Go to official source first (annual report, official blog, keynote)
2. Cross-reference with one credible secondary source
3. Look for the PROCESS, not the outcome
4. Stop when you have one extractable workflow

### For Top Earner Topics
1. Find the named practitioner's documented work (book, podcast, documented case)
2. Extract the system they run, not the results they claim
3. Look for evidence from their clients, not just their claims
4. Stop when you have one verified workflow

### For Sports Branding Topics
1. Use official league/team sources + Sports Business Journal
2. Find the specific campaign, program, or system
3. Apply the translation table from SPORTS_BRANDING_FRAMEWORK.md
4. Stop when you have one Gabriel-applicable insight

## When to Stop Researching

Stop when:
- You have ONE clear finding with ONE source you can cite
- The 20-minute limit is reached
- You've found contradictory evidence that cancels the hypothesis

Do NOT keep searching to confirm a hypothesis. That's confirmation bias.

## What to Hand Off to Evidence Judge

Pass to `evidence-judge.md`:
- The finding (1 sentence)
- The source (specific citation)
- Your evidence level assessment (1–5)
- Your classification (fact | inference | pattern | hypothesis)

Do not pass vague summaries. Pass specific claims with sources.
