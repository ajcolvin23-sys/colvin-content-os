# Top Earner Research Framework

## Core Principle

Don't study elite operators for motivation. Study them for repeatable process.

The question is never "What did they achieve?" The question is always: "What exact process did they run, and can Gabriel run a version of it for Alfred?"

---

## Who to Study

### AI Consultants and Automation Agencies
- Solo operators running $500K–$2M productized AI services
- AI agencies with named pricing models and public case studies
- Operators who have documented their client acquisition process

### Marketing Consultants and Copywriters
- Practitioners with documented results (not just followers)
- Conversion specialists (CXL graduates, named A/B test results)
- Direct response practitioners with verifiable client wins

### Content Creators (Business / Education)
- Creators with 100K–5M subscribers in business, money, or education
- Creators who have documented their content system publicly
- Podcast operators with named monetization strategies

### Coaches and Advisors
- Financial coaches with documented client transformations
- Business coaches with named frameworks
- Faith-aligned business leaders (relevant to Alfred's brand values)

### Agency Operators
- Marketing agencies with public pricing and service docs
- Productized service operators (no custom scope)
- Local business marketing specialists with case studies

### Local Business Champions
- Best-reviewed service businesses in Indianapolis
- Multi-location service operators in Indiana
- Church and faith-based organization leaders with operational excellence

---

## Study Framework (6 Lenses)

### 1. Client Acquisition Process
- Where do their clients come from? (content, referrals, outreach, ads, partnerships)
- What is their lead magnet or entry offer?
- What does their sales conversation look like?
- What objections do they overcome?
- How does Alfred position against them?

### 2. Offer Architecture
- What is their core offer and price point?
- What do they include / exclude?
- How do they scope the work to protect profitability?
- What productization system do they use?
- What offer of Alfred's competes or complements this?

### 3. Delivery System
- How do they fulfill at scale without burning out?
- What is their client onboarding process?
- What do they delegate vs. keep?
- What AI tools do they use?
- What delivery workflow can Gabriel extract?

### 4. Content and Authority System
- What content do they produce weekly?
- What platform do they prioritize?
- How do they repurpose content?
- What authority signal drives their credibility?
- What can Alfred adapt for his content engine?

### 5. Pricing Psychology
- What price anchors do they use?
- How do they present ROI?
- What does their pricing page say?
- How do they handle "it's too expensive"?
- What pricing lesson applies to Alfred?

### 6. Retention and Referral
- How do they keep clients beyond the first engagement?
- What is their referral engine?
- What community do they build?
- What can Alfred implement in his CRM follow-up?

---

## Extraction Rules

1. Document the process, not just the result.
2. Cite where you found this — don't rely on reputation alone.
3. Separate their claimed process from their observed process.
4. Flag if their system only works at their scale (5,000 email subscribers ≠ Alfred's current scale).
5. Always end with what Alfred can run tomorrow vs. what requires 6 months to build.
