# Top Company Research Framework

## Core Principle

Don't study companies for inspiration. Study them for extractable workflows.

Every elite company analysis must end with: "Here is the exact process Gabriel can run."

## Study Framework (7 Lenses)

For each company, ask these 7 questions:

### 1. Customer Acquisition
- How do they get their first 100 customers? First 10,000?
- What channels do they dominate?
- What is their unfair acquisition advantage?
- What can Alfred copy at his scale?

### 2. Brand Positioning
- What is the one thing they stand for?
- What do they refuse to be?
- How do they create an identity beyond the product?
- What language do they own?

### 3. Product Strategy
- What is the "working backwards" model they use?
- How do they decide what NOT to build?
- How do they sequence launches?
- What applies to Alfred's products?

### 4. Pricing & Distribution
- How do they price for trust vs. premium vs. volume?
- What is their distribution moat?
- What flywheel do they run?
- Which pricing lesson applies to Alfred's offers?

### 5. Retention & Loyalty
- How do they keep customers for 5+ years?
- What is their community strategy?
- What does their CX system look like?
- What can Gabriel replicate in Alfred's follow-up?

### 6. Operational Excellence
- What process did they standardize that others do manually?
- What did they automate first?
- How do they measure quality?
- What workflow can Gabriel extract?

### 7. AI & Data Strategy
- How are they using AI to reduce costs or speed up output?
- What data do they collect that others ignore?
- What AI use case from them applies to Alfred's automation offers?

## Extraction Rules

1. Cite the source. Do not invent company practices.
2. Distinguish between what they say they do vs. what data shows they do.
3. Label inferences as inferences.
4. Extract the process, not the inspiration.
5. Adapt to Alfred's scale — not Amazon's $500B budget.
6. One workflow per analysis. Not ten.
