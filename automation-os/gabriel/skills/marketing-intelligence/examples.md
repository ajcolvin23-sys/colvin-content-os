# Marketing Intelligence — Examples

## Example 1: Good — Pain-First Email Subject Lines

**Question:** What email subject line structures get the highest open rates for service businesses?
**Source:** Klaviyo Email Benchmark Report 2025 + Copyhackers documented test (High tier)

**Finding:** Subject lines that name a specific pain ("Still tracking clients in spreadsheets?") outperform generic subject lines ("How to grow your business") by 34% average open rate.

**Evidence Level:** 3 — Specific data from platform report + named practitioner
**Classification:** Pattern (across multiple industries in the benchmark)

**Alfred's Application:** For Colvin Enterprises outreach emails, open every subject line with the prospect's most likely specific pain, not a generic benefit claim.

**Channel:** Email
**Alfred's Voice Check:** PASS — the warmth and specificity match Alfred's style

**Proposed Skill Update:** Add to `content-engine/SKILL.md` under email subject lines section.

## Example 2: Bad — Generic Advice Loop

**What happened:** Gabriel researched "social media marketing" and produced: "Post consistently, engage with your audience, and use hashtags."

**Why it failed:** The question was too broad. The finding is generic advice that appears in every social media guide. No evidence cited. No specific mechanism extracted.

**Rule reinforced:** Questions must be specific. Not "social media marketing" — "Which TikTok video format has the highest completion rate for educational content about home buying?"
