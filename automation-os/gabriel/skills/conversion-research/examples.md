# Conversion Research — Examples

## Example 1: Good Run

**Question:** What CTA label outperforms "Submit" on lead capture forms?
**Source:** Formisimo (acquired by Zuko) documented analysis of 50k+ form interactions + CXL article citing the same data (High tier)

**Finding:** Action-oriented CTA labels ("Get My Free Guide" vs. "Submit") improved form completions by an average of 18% across service business lead forms in the cited studies.

**Evidence Level:** 3 — credible, named platform data
**Classification:** Pattern (across multiple forms, not a single controlled test)

**Alfred's Application:** For First Keys Indy contact form — change "Submit" to "Send My Request" or "Get the Free Buyer Guide." Run as A/B test before permanent change.

**Mobile Applicability:** Yes — tested on both mobile and desktop in source.

**Proposed Experiment:** Change First Keys Indy form CTA from "Submit" to "Get the Free Buyer Guide." Track form fill rate over 14 days minimum.

## Example 2: Bad — Wrong Source Tier

**What happened:** Gabriel cited a Twitter thread claiming "red CTA buttons always outperform green ones" as conversion evidence.

**Why it failed:** Social media claim, no data, no named test. Level 1 evidence at best.

**Rule reinforced:** CTA color research requires actual A/B test data from a named study. The correct source is CXL or a named brand's documented test.
