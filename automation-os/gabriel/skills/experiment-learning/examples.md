# Experiment Learning — Examples

## Example 1: Winner Declared Correctly

**EXP-001: First Keys Indy form CTA**
Control: "Submit" | Variant: "Get the Free Buyer Guide"
Primary metric: Form fill rate
Data period: 21 days | Visitors: 340

**Result:** Form fill rate improved from 3.2% (control) to 5.1% (variant) — 59% improvement, well above the 15% threshold.

**Winner:** Variant — "Get the Free Buyer Guide"

**Lesson (WHY):** The variant communicates what the person receives, not what they have to do. Action-oriented + benefit-forward = lower friction. This matches the CXL research that informed the hypothesis.

**Actions:**
1. Apply "Get the Free Buyer Guide" as the permanent CTA
2. Update `website-cro/SKILL.md` — add rule: "For lead capture forms, CTA labels should name the benefit received, not the action taken"
3. Propose to approved-learnings.md

## Example 2: Inconclusive Result

**EXP-002: Colvin Enterprises headline test**
Ran for 14 days, 180 visitors. Primary metric: "Book a call" click rate.
Control: 2.1% | Variant: 2.3% — only 9.5% improvement, below the 15% threshold.

**Winner:** Inconclusive

**Action:** Control maintained. Archived both versions. Note that headline change alone may not be sufficient — consider testing full hero section as next experiment.
