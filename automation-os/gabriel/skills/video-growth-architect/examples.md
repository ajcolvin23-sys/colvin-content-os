## Example 3 — Deep Mode Hook Ranking, Music Theory Secrets

```
HOOK RANKING (Deep Mode)

Ranked by: scroll-stop potential × brand-fit × compliance safety

1. "You can play hundreds of songs with 4 chords." — 9.2/10
   Why: Specific number + concrete claim + immediately curious
   
2. "Piano isn't random. It's patterns." — 8.8/10
   Why: Directly challenges the audience's belief; very short
   
3. "If chords confuse you, watch this." — 8.5/10
   Why: Invites the confused learner; works as text and audio
   
4. "Church musicians: learn this chord shortcut." — 8.3/10
   Why: Hyper-targeted niche; church musicians are highly engaged
   
5. "They told you piano takes years. It doesn't have to." — 8.0/10
   Why: Challenges authority belief; inspirational but not overclaiming
   
6. "Stop memorizing songs. Learn the system." — 7.8/10
   Why: Clear identity statement; speaks to the frustrated memorizer
   
7. "Your chords sound basic because you need the number system." — 7.5/10
   Why: Specific diagnosis; works for intermediate players
   
8. "This 4-chord pattern unlocks modern music." — 7.2/10
   Why: Intriguing but "unlocks modern music" is broad
   
9. "Want to play by ear? Learn this first." — 7.0/10
   Why: Good CTA framing but not as surprising as top hooks
   
10. "Most beginners quit because nobody teaches patterns." — 6.8/10
    Why: Relatable but slightly passive; frame could be sharper
```
