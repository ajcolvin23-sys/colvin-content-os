# Sports Branding Analysis — Examples

## Example 1: Good Run — WNBA Fan Engagement

**Organization:** WNBA
**System:** Fan base growth playbook — community building
**Source:** Sports Business Journal article documenting WNBA's 2024 viewership growth + official WNBA social media strategy blog (High tier)

**Finding:** The WNBA's 2023–2024 growth was driven by player-first storytelling — teams documented player personalities, struggles, and off-court life, then let fans become emotionally invested before the season started. Viewership grew 48% YoY.

**Evidence Level:** 3 — documented in Sports Business Journal with named data
**Classification:** Pattern (multi-team, multi-season, measured result)

**Translation (Sports → Alfred):**
The "player-first" approach = "founder-first" approach for Alfred's brands.
Don't lead with Girls Got Game's program features. Lead with the stories of individual girls — their challenges, growth, and moments of success. Let the audience invest in the people before they invest in the program.

**Girls Got Game Application:**
Content arc strategy: 8-week social content series featuring individual athlete stories before the next season/event. Each post is one girl's story. No program selling until Week 7–8.

**Extracted Workflow:**
1. Identify 4–6 athletes/participants willing to share their stories
2. Conduct a simple video interview or written Q&A
3. Schedule one story per week for 6 weeks
4. At Week 7: introduce the program/event as the vehicle for more stories like these
5. At Week 8: CTA — register, donate, or sponsor

## Example 2: Scale Mismatch Note

**What happened:** Gabriel studied the NFL's $100M+ community outreach program and suggested Alfred replicate it for Girls Got Game.

**Why it failed:** NFL runs community programs with dedicated staff, grant funding, and league-level resources. The principle (community = fanbase = loyalty) is transferable; the mechanism is not.

**Rule added:** Always assess: "What is the principle here?" Extract the principle, not the infrastructure.
