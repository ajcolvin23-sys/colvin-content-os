# GABRIEL — PLATFORM ENGAGEMENT RESEARCH LOOP

## Purpose

Gabriel researches what is currently working across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, Facebook Reels, and LinkedIn video — then translates those patterns into brand-safe video strategies for First Keys Indy, Colvin Enterprises, and Music Theory Secrets.

**Gabriel must use this skill before:**
- Major video campaigns (4+ videos)
- Monthly content batches
- Ad tests
- New Remotion template builds
- Hook library refreshes

**Gabriel does NOT need this skill for:**
- Single daily posts (use existing hook library)
- Quick content rewrites
- Compliance reviews

---

## Core Research Question

> What video structures, hooks, topics, captions, sounds, visuals, and CTAs are currently producing engagement on each platform for this brand's audience?

### Step 1 — Define Brand Context

Before searching, lock in:

```

```

### Step 2 — Search Current Content

Find current examples related to:
- Audience pain points and language
- Offer category and closest alternatives
- Local market (Indianapolis when relevant)
- Competitor messaging and positioning
- Viral hooks in the niche
- Common video formats repeated across accounts
- Comments showing buyer pain, questions, frustration
- Patterns appearing on 3+ similar accounts

### Step 3 — Extract Winning Patterns

For each platform, identify:
- Best hook types (question? stat? contradiction? story?)
- Best first-frame styles (face cam? text overlay? product? result?)
- Best video lengths (5s? 15s? 25s? 60s?)
- Common caption styles (short + CTA? long story? hashtag-heavy?)
- Common CTA patterns ("comment X," "link in bio," "DM me," "watch part 2")
- Editing/motion style (fast cuts? one-take? text-only? kinetic?)
- Sound usage (trending audio? original? voice-only? silence?)
- Visual pacing (cuts per second, zoom frequency, text timing)

### Step 4 — Score Each Pattern

Rate each pattern 1–10 on:

| Factor | What it measures |
|---|---|
| Relevance to brand | Does it fit our voice, audience, and offer? |
| Ease of production | Can Gabriel + Remotion execute this? |
| Conversion potential | Does it lead toward the CTA? |
| Trust safety | Is it honest, not hype? |
| Platform fit | Is this native to the platform? |
| Novelty | Is this fresher than what we're currently doing? |
| Repeatability | Can we use this format weekly? |

**Minimum score to add to test queue: 35/70 total.**

### Step 5 — Translate Into Brand-Specific Ideas

For every useful pattern, translate it into all three brands.

**Example translation:**
```
Trend Pattern:
"POV: You thought you couldn't afford it, then you found the missing step."

First Keys Indy:
"POV: You thought the down payment stopped you, then you checked your eligibility."

Colvin Enterprises:
"POV: You thought you needed more employees, but you needed better workflows."

Music Theory Secrets:
"POV: You thought chords were random, then you learned the number system."
```

**Rule:** Never copy a creator or brand directly. Extract:
- The structural pattern (POV, question, stat, contradiction)
- The emotional trigger (FOMO, frustration, hope, curiosity)
- The visual pacing concept
- The CTA logic (soft CTA vs. hard CTA vs. engagement-first)

Then produce original content that serves Alfred's audience and offer.

## Platform-Specific Research Priorities

### TikTok
Study:
- Hook types producing 3-second hold rate lift
- Trending audio in the niche (education, real estate, music)
- Fast caption pacing patterns
- Comment-triggering framing ("Tell me if this is you...")
- Native vs. polished aesthetic split

Success signals: 3-second hold, full-video completion, comment volume, saves

### Instagram Reels
Study:
- Save-worthy educational formats
- Aesthetic consistency patterns
- First-frame visual style (text overlay vs. talking head vs. graphic)
- Caption length and structure (short + CTA vs. long story)
- How top creators drive profile visits from Reels

Success signals: saves, shares, profile visits, follower conversion

### YouTube Shorts
Study:
- Searchable hook phrasing (starts with a keyword or question)
- Curiosity loop structures (tease → teach → CTA)
- Retention patterns (when do viewers drop off?)
- Series format opportunities (part 1, part 2, etc.)
- Comment-driving question at end

Success signals: retention %, replays, comments, channel subscriber conversion

### Facebook Reels
Study:
- Local and community-first content patterns
- Share triggers (practical, relatable, useful)
- Slower pacing compared to TikTok — what still works?
- Trust-first vs. entertainment-first
- Indianapolis/Marion County local relevance signals

Success signals: shares, comments, link clicks, event RSVPs

### LinkedIn Video
Study:
- Business pain framing (specific, not generic)
- Credibility signals (data, case studies, experience)
- Under-30-second format for awareness plays
- Strong CTA in both video AND post copy
- Founder/operator authenticity vs. polished brand content

Success signals: profile views, booked calls, post engagement, connection requests
