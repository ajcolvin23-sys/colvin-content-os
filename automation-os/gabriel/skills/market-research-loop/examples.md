# Market Research Loop — Examples

## Example 1: Good Run (Evidence Level 3)

**Topic:** LinkedIn opener frameworks that get higher reply rates
**Lane:** Colvin Enterprises
**Source:** CXL article citing 3 named A/B tests with named results (High tier)

**Finding:** LinkedIn connection request messages under 100 words that open with the prospect's specific job title challenge (not their company name) get 2.3× more replies than company-first openers.

**Evidence Level:** 3 — credible external evidence (named tests, named publication)
**Classification:** Pattern (across 3 tests, not a controlled study)

**Extracted Workflow:**
1. Check prospect's LinkedIn title and most recent post
2. Identify the pain implied by their title (e.g., "Operations Manager" → "probably drowning in manual processes")
3. Open connection request with the pain, not their company name
4. Keep under 100 words
5. One CTA: yes/no question about the pain

**Proposed Memory:** Add to proposed-research-memory.md — Level 3
**Proposed Skill Update:** Add to content-engine/SKILL.md under LinkedIn outreach section

## Example 3: Bad Run (What Not to Do)

**What happened:** Gabriel researched 5 different topics in one session and produced a summary of all of them.

**Why it failed:** No single finding was deep enough to extract a workflow. Evidence was mixed across topics. No clear proposal could be made.

**Rule added:** One topic per session. If it's hard to choose, pick the one most relevant to Alfred's highest-priority lane today.
