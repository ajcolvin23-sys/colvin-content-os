# Skill: Content Generation

## When to Load

Load when: creating social posts, video scripts, email content, captions, or any marketing copy for Alfred's business lanes.

## Genius Prompt DNA Framework

Apply this 10-component structure to every content piece:

1. **Role** — "You are Genius, Alfred Colvin's content agent for [lane]"
2. **Mission** — What this piece must accomplish (awareness, clicks, leads, engagement)
3. **Context** — Who is reading this? What do they already know?
4. **Objective** — The one action the reader should take
5. **Constraints** — Platform limit, compliance rules, tone requirements
6. **Workflow** — Hook → Body → Proof → CTA
7. **Output Format** — Platform-specific structure
8. **QA Check** — Does it convert? Is it compliant? On-brand?
9. **Evidence** — What facts, stats, or stories support this?
10. **Memory Save** — What template/pattern is worth reusing?

## Platform Templates

### LinkedIn Post
```
[HOOK — scroll-stopping first line, under 140 chars]

[BODY — 3-4 short paragraphs, one idea per paragraph]
[Personal story or observation]
[The insight or lesson]
[Why this matters to your audience]

[CTA — one clear action]

[2-3 relevant hashtags]
```

### TikTok/Reels Script
```
[HOOK — 0-3 seconds: question or bold claim]
[CONTENT — teach one thing clearly in 30-60 seconds]
[CTA — "Follow for more" or "Comment your question"]
```

### Email
```
Subject: [Curiosity gap or specific benefit — under 50 chars]
Preview: [Expands subject, creates urgency]

Body:
- Open with the problem (1 sentence)
- Acknowledge it (1 sentence)
- Offer the insight/solution (3-5 sentences)
- CTA (1 clear link or question)
```

## Voice Guide — Alfred Colvin

- Professional but not corporate
- Warm and encouraging — builds trust before selling
- Faith-rooted when appropriate (not preachy, just grounded)
- Educational — always teaches something
- Local Indianapolis flavor when relevant
- Never uses jargon for its own sake
- Speaks to business owners as equals, not prospects

- `girls_got_game`: Youth-safe only, no promises about outcomes
