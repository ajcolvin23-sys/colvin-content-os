# Gabriel Brand Memory Policy — Colvin Content OS

Brand voice, approved messaging, audience profiles, and campaign learnings. Every content agent loads this before generating.

---

## Alfred's Master Brand Voice

**Tone:** Professional + warm + educational + faith-rooted. Never corporate, never cold, never hype-bro.
**Style:** Clear, direct, specific. No fluff. Real examples. Real results.
**Faith element:** Natural, not preachy. Faith informs Alfred's worldview — it doesn't dominate every sentence.
**Audience respect:** Alfred treats his audience as intelligent adults who want value, not manipulation.
**What Alfred does NOT sound like:** Salesy, desperate, generic, buzzword-heavy, inauthentic.

## Per-Lane Brand Guidelines

### Colvin Enterprises (AI Automation Consulting)
- **Voice:** Strategic advisor, not a vendor. Problem-solver.
- **Audience:** Small business owners, CEOs, pastors, coaches, nonprofit leaders in Indianapolis area
- **Tone:** Confident, specific, results-oriented. "Here's what AI can do for your business, specifically."
- **Key messages:** Save time, reduce manual work, grow without adding headcount, compete like a bigger company
- **Do say:** "15 hours saved per week," "automated follow-up," "AI agent for your specific workflow"
- **Don't say:** "AI will change everything," "disrupt your industry," generic AI hype

### Indiana Backflow Directory
- **Voice:** Local utility resource. Trusted local directory.
- **Audience:** Property managers, facilities directors, commercial building owners, plumbers, HOAs, churches, schools
- **Tone:** Practical, local-authority, compliance-helpful
- **Key messages:** "Find a certified Indiana backflow tester," "stay compliant," "92 counties covered"
- **Do say:** County-specific information, certification facts, "your local tester"
- **Don't say:** Over-promise on tester quality or certification (directory only, not endorsement)

### Music Theory Secrets
- **Voice:** Gospel piano mentor. Enthusiastic teacher. Insider knowledge sharer.
- **Audience:** Church musicians, gospel pianists, worship leaders, music students, self-taught players
- **Tone:** Exciting, revelatory, educational. "You're about to learn what took me years to figure out."
- **Key messages:** "Secret chords," "gospel voicings," "play by ear," "improvise like a pro"
- **Product:** Music Theory Secrets book — link in every post CTA
- **Do say:** Specific chord names, gospel music references, "your pastor will notice the difference"
- **Don't say:** Vague musical promises without specific techniques

### First Keys Indy
- **Voice:** Trusted homebuyer advocate. Community resource. Not a salesperson.
- **Audience:** First-time homebuyers in Marion County. Often working-class, diverse, skeptical of too-good-to-be-true claims.
- **Tone:** Warm, honest, empowering. "You may qualify for help you don't know about."
- **Key messages:** DPA availability, eligibility basics, how to start, what the process looks like
- **Compliance requirement:** No guaranteed approval language. No specific unverified dollar amounts.
- **Do say:** "Many buyers in Marion County qualify for down payment assistance"
- **Don't say:** "Get free money," "guaranteed," "no credit check needed"

### FundingReady Indiana
- **Voice:** Grant navigator. Indiana business resource expert.
- **Audience:** Small business owners, nonprofits, rural businesses, women/minority-owned businesses in Indiana
- **Tone:** Practical, optimistic, knowledgeable. "This funding exists and you can access it."
- **Key messages:** Grant availability, how to apply, what qualifies, free checklist
- **Do say:** Specific grant names and sources
- **Don't say:** "We guarantee you'll get the grant" — always caveat with eligibility requirements

### Girls Got Game
- **Voice:** Youth sports empowerment. Parent-facing. Coach-friendly.
- **Audience:** Parents of girls ages 8-18, youth sports coaches, school administrators in Indiana
- **Tone:** Energetic, positive, community-focused
- **Key messages:** Leadership through sport, confidence building, teamwork, Indiana youth programs
- **Non-negotiable:** Youth-safe content only. No personal data about minors.

### GloryEngine / Yahweh Comics
- **Voice:** Faith-rooted creative. Biblical foundation. Creative storytelling.
- **Audience:** Christians, faith media consumers, comic book fans who are faith-aligned, church communities
- **Tone:** Reverent but accessible. Not preachy — inspiring.
- **Key messages:** Faith through visual storytelling, biblical truth in modern format
- **Non-negotiable:** Content must align with Alfred's Christian faith values

## Brand Voice Anti-Patterns (Never Use)

- "Game-changer" or "disrupting"
- "Passive income" without context
- Excessive exclamation marks (max 1 per post)
- ALL CAPS (except acronyms)
- "Secret formula" without backing it up
- Fake scarcity ("Only 3 spots left!") unless literally true
- Generic stock photo captions
- Content that makes Alfred sound like someone else's template

## Past Campaign Learnings

These are updated as campaigns run and Alfred provides feedback:
- Video with "secret chord" hook outperforms generic "gospel piano lesson"
- First Keys Indy Facebook posts with real homebuyer outcome stories get high engagement
- Indianapolis LinkedIn posts that name-drop local businesses or institutions outperform generic ones
- Short (30-45 second) Remotion videos outperform longer explainers for TikTok
- Gospel music content performs best Thursday/Friday (pre-worship weekend energy)
